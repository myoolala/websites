E91 n52 clutch replacement

Date: July 3, 2025 Chassis: E91 Trim: 328i msport Time taken: 3 hours Number of people: 2 Engine: N52 Difficulty: medium
Step-by-step guide

The process we went through in order to change the clutch on the car. Overall this was a way easier job than expected. I did this job because I fucked up and installed the pressure plate wrong 2 months prior.

Lift the car

Recommend doing this on at least a mid-rise lift as the transmission is reasonably tall and you'll want the room

Remove the splash shields

These will be 8 and 10mm sockets

Disconnect the exhaust

You might need fire and new hardware for this depending the age of the bolts.

Note: If you can soak these bolts in penetrator the night before or be ready to break them.

Remove the clutch slave cylinder

This will be under some tension when you remove it so be careful.

Disconnect the driveshaft

Specifically from the transmission. Once done, remove the guibo you can push the driveshaft into its yoke a few inches. This will give you enough room to pull the transmission.

Disconnect the shifter bracket and linkage

You do not need to pull the shift out from in the car. Instead, unclip the linkage at the selector shaft and unclip the bracket from the transmission. A right angle pick makes this job easy.

Remove the wire bracket from the transmission

This will get in the way when pulling the transmission. Remove the harness from it, disconnect the ground from the oil level sensor, and then remove the bracket from the car. Also remove all of the small e10 bell housing bolts at the bottom.

Remove the transmission cross member and mounts

You don't need a jack for this. You have so much torque from the back of the transmission to lift it up yourself and remove it. Once removed, you can let it go and the engine will balance itself forward.

Unclip the wiring harness from the transmission

There are more clips holding wires on the driver's side of the transmission and on top of the transmission. First unplug the reverse switch and then unclip the wires from the transmission. You should have the oil level sensor wires, secondary 02 sensor wires, and reverse switch wire dangling by now.

Remove the 3 top bell housing bolts

Have fun with those extensions and wobbles. There will be 2 into the engine and then the long bolt going into the starter. This is why we need a set of starter bolts when doing this job.

Note: The engine can flex a lot on the mounts, we used that to our advantage

Remove the 4 large bell housing bolts

e16s... I think. These will be the last bolts holding the tranmission on. Be ready to support the transmission in case it releases on its own.

Remove the transmission

It's not too heavy, definitely very doable with only 1 person. You might need to turn the output shaft to help motivate it. Once it moves it will pull right out.

Note: If you need a mallet for more motivation, thread in one of the large bolts to catch it.

Remove the pressure plate and clutch

You can just zip the 6 bolts out. Unfortunately, when this is removed it will mess with the self adjusting mechanism. I've talked to a couple shops and sthey say you can't reset the mechanism yourself, but I've seen somethings online that show how. idk what to believe, I never tried it though.

Replace parts while off the car

Namely, the luk kit comes with a throw out bearing and fork. I also recommend replacing the shift selector seal as it will never be easier. You can also replace the pilot bearing and rear main seal. To do either requires removing the flywheel. Also inspect the flywheel for play axially and rotationally.

Install the new clutch and pressure plate

Ok, first start by taking the alignment tool and pulling out the center section via its screw. Don't unscrew it, use the screw to pull it out. Then you can put the tool into the clutch and pilot bearing. The pressure plate will press onto this tool and that's fine. Torque the pressure plate to spec

Remove the scary spider

This spider if removed prior to installation will undo the self adjusting mechanism. You must wait until the pressure plate is torqued down before removing this. Use a 17mm socket to remove this. Then remove the alighment tool.

Put the car back together

Steps 1 through 12 in reverse.
