3rd track weekend for the e91

Date: May 02-04, 2025 Weather: 85°F · Clear
What changed since the last track day

I decided to lock the oil thermostat open to see what the max cooling I could get would do. I also updated to adjustable length and quite a bit beefier front end links since those have broken on track twice now. I did have to get new tires last second, chords were showing. Oh also, I'm on my second diff now since the first one blew up in April.

Oil temps

Compared to March, oil temps were definitely higher, saw a peak of about 250. All together liveable. I would love to see closer to 230 but I'm unsure how to get there about this point. But, it was a pretty warm day on a power track in the sun.

Car speed

This car is fast son, at least for a 3700lb station wagon on mild tires. Keeps ups with all of the e36 m3s just fine, well except for the caged ones. Saw peak indicated speed of about 130mph on the main straight. Basically, the top of 5th.

The brakes

Delightful, never a hint of brake fade and I was about as mean as I could be. Could not be happier. The bit so hard with great ramp up. Definitely out braked a few cars

Shock and springs

Hilarious as always. I can basically jump curbs. More over, learned a lot more about timing my inputs with weight transfer. This setup makes it very obvious the effects so I got to play with using shifts and other more nuanced forms of weight transfer to adjust the car. More over, learning to let the car do car things.

Balance

Perfect. 2.1° camber up front, 1.8 in the rear, squaure toe up front, 1/32" in on the rear. The m3 bar made this car love to rotate in a slow and progressive fashion. Made turn in fun.

Skidpad

Again one of the easiest cars I have ever driven on skidpad. Left with a perfect score and to quote my instructor: "I was the student this time.". I practiced holding slides through deep puddles, holding full lock with speeds as low as possible, etc... Jeff wanted to write an article on the car after driving it.

Kaboom

Going into turn 5 about about 90mph, cylinder 6 disintegrated. What's incredible is that even after it went down to 5 cylinders, the engine on its own revved to redline. You can hear it in the video. Amazing engine.

Overall

Probably my most fun track car. It's a trank that lets you use all 6 gears on track. More sideways action than my m2cs

Trip summary

  • Date: May 02-04, 2025
  • Distance: ~300 mi on track miles
  • Duration: 3 days
  • Average temp: 70-85°F
  • Avg MPG: 7.8

Car setup

  • Chassis: E91
  • Suspension: b6 shocks, stock springs, m3 sways bars
  • Tires: 255/40R17 Firestone Indy500
  • Brake setup: 335i brakes Hawk5.0 up front, blue in the rear
