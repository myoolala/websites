Replace all calipers, rotors, and brake pads

Date: Nov 9, 2023 Chassis: E91 Trim: 328i msport Engine: N52 Difficulty: easy-medium
Step-by-step guide

This is a basic DIY log for changing the oil, spark plugs, and ignition coils on the 3 Series. Always work on a cool engine, use proper jack stands, and follow torque specs from factory documentation.

Prepare the car and gather tools

Park the car on level ground, set the parking brake, and chock the rear wheels. Lay out your tools, new oil, filter, plugs, and coils so everything is within reach before you lift the car.

Tools Safety
Tip: snap a quick photo of the engine bay and coil harness routing before you unplug anything. It makes reassembly easier.

Lift the front and drain the oil

Jack up the front of the car and support it securely on jack stands. Remove the undertray if needed to access the drain plug. Place a drain pan, then remove the oil fill cap and carefully loosen the drain plug. Let the oil drain completely.

Underbody Oil Mess warning

Replace the oil filter

With the engine still draining, move to the oil filter housing. Remove the cap, swap the filter element, and replace any O-rings that come with the new filter. Lightly coat new O-rings with fresh oil before reinstalling.

Oil filter
Note: pay attention to the orientation of the old filter and the plastic cage, if present, so the new one seats correctly.

Access coils & remove old spark plugs

Remove the engine cover and any necessary trim to access the ignition coils. Unplug each coil connector, then pull the coil out. Using a spark plug socket, carefully remove the old plugs without cross-threading. Inspect them and note any unusual wear or oil fouling.

Ignition Spark plugs

Install new plugs & coils

Gap the new plugs if required and thread them in by hand first. Torque them to spec from the service manual. Install the new coils, ensuring each one is fully seated and the electrical connector clicks into place.

Torque spec New parts

Refill oil, check for leaks, and reset

Reinstall the oil drain plug with a new crush washer, torque to spec, and reinstall the undertray. Lower the car, then fill with the correct amount and weight of oil. Start the engine, check for leaks, and verify level once the car has run for a minute. Reset the oil service indicator if applicable.

Final checks Service reset
Log: note mileage, date, oil brand/weight, and part numbers so you can track intervals in your build or maintenance log.
