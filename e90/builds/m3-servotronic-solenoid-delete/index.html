Delete m3 servotronic solenoid

Date: Aug 14, 2025 Chassis: E91 Trim: 328i msport Engine: N52 Difficulty: very-easy
Step-by-step guide

Removing the servotrnoic solenoid in favor of the HydraPerformance delete plate.

Prepare the car and gather tools

The m3 steering rack comes with a solenoid for servotronic that adjust steering weight dynamically. This allows the car to lighten the weight at parking lot speeds and weighten it for stability at speed. My car does not have the wiring for this system which defaults it to the heaviest mode always.

Raise the car

Either on jack stands or via a lift, raise the car

Remove that splash shield

This is the only thing blocking access to the solenoid.

Remove the solenoid

This is just 2 Torx bits. Then the solenoid just pulls right out. This will cause the reservoir to dain. Have a small pan ready to catch the fluid.

Wait for the fluid to drain

I mean, unless you like being dirty

Installed the block off plate

Lube up the block off plate o-rings with power steering fluid and seat it into the rack. Install the 2 screws and torque them to the proper torque spec that I am too lazy to find while typing this.

Reinstall the splash shield

All those lovely little 8mm

Refill and bleed the power steering system

With the car on the ground, fill the power steering reservoir. Then have a friend go in the car, start it, and begin turning the wheel lock to lock. At least 10 times. While that is happening, continue to fill the reservoir as needed

Job summary

  • Date: Aug 14, 2025
  • Tools: jack + stands, 8mm, Torx bits, allen keys, torque wrench
  • Parts: Hydra performance block off plate, power steering fluid
