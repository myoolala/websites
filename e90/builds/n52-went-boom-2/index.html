WE BLEW UP ANOTHER N52

Date: Nov 02, 2025
Context

For starters, this is not my car, but a friends 128i that we been building before my wagon. This was the last autocross of the year and my goal was to have fun and push the cars. The 128i was finally ready to drive hopefully without mechanicals on some fresh sticky tires. The track itself is an old one with not a lot of grip.

Before it blew

2ish? idk I left for the day by there

Our group we tend to share cars a lot because why not. For this car with the tires, this helped get heat in the tires. The car was getting hotlapped. A check engine light came on before the run the engine blew on.

Optional photo: the car in the garage before heading out

Lock up

Again, idk.

My friend gets in his car, and within 15 seconds of the run on a long left sweeper, the engine locks. My friend doesn't know if the wheels locked but he knows he lost power steering before coming to a stop. It was a very sudden lock up and obviously the car wouldn't start.

Note: logged a few minutes of data here — speed, steering angle, and ride height changes — to compare against alignment day runs.

First step in diagnosis

The next day

Car home we started diving into little by little. Check to confirm just the car rolls in gear with the clutch in to confirm the transmission isn't to blame. Remove the belt too. Nothing. Putting a breaker bar on the crank and it doesn't move, not even millimeters. It is very stuck even putting what was probably a few hundred pounds of force into the bar. After that, the transmission and flywheel got pulled once again to confirm that wasn't the issue. Nope.

The block was also free of any damage. No particals in the filter or vanos screens. Aside from the crank not turning, there were no symptoms.

Optional photo: overlook / car parked with the mountains in the background

Pulled the motor

At this point we pulled the motor, out the bottom which was WAY easier than the front like what I did. Still no signs

Road notes: corner 3 tight hairpin, slight off-camber; car stayed neutral with just a hint of safe understeer when pushing harder.

Pulling the engine apart we found the culprits. Look at these rod bearings.

3 spun bears, 3 more heat damaged bearings, and a bent crank. Very nice. What is interesting is the lack of heat marks i nthe connecting rods indicating a very sudden failure/loss of oil pressure. The mains were spotless though

Theories on why

Talking with a friend who raced bmw's professionally, it seems that this was a combination of issues. The failure was obviously a lack of oil. The concensus landed on we are asking too much of the stock engine. Namely, this engine was designed back in the early 2000s for not motorsport use. Couple that with 25 year newer very sticky rubber and an aggressive sport to slosh oil with an aggresive driver. We lost oil pressure and fried the motor

Fix?

All together, installing the new motor is easy so nothing to talk about there. What we did do was the same thing as I did on mine already. Installed an oil pan baffle. The n54 one welds right into the alluminum pans without issue. Not a guaranteed fix but definitely worth it and we will be installing these on all motors now.


I plan to add an accusump to my car now.
