Replacing the engine post track day.

Date: May 20, 2025 Difficulty: hard Estimated time: 2 long day
What happened

So on the 3rd day on track for the weekend my car decided it no longer needed cylinder 6. Cylinder 6 was pretty offended by that and proceeded rip 2 holes in the block.

Pulling the motor it appears a rod bolt backed out causing the immediate and catastrophic failure. Talking with a rep from BMW this is a known thing, more of an issue on the v8s and v10s, but the i6 engines are not immune from it. Who knew. Feel free to look at all of the pictures



Step-by-step guide

This won't be as much as a step by step guide but a generalized guide of what I did and had to buy.

Removed the old engine

This was pretty easy, I pulled the bumper and front clip for this. I did that mostly because I got good at doing it over the winter and had to remove the bumper anyway for the oil cooler. This total took about 3 hours, pulling the engine included

Pulled the engine apart and kept the good parts

Namely the intakes, headers, milvs, etc... those sorts of things I wanted to transfer over

I received the new motor from ebay

I had to have it shipped to a commercial address but it was $2400 for a 46k mileage motor from an xi auto

Bought parts

Have fun with this list. I bought more but this is the minimum. I also did coolant hoses, more rubber, etc...

  • Valve cover gasket kit
  • Intake manifold gaskets
  • OFHG
  • Oil Thermostat gasket
  • Oil cooler o-rings
  • Exhaust gaskets
  • Oil pan bolt set
  • Oil pump bolt set - this is to convert the oil pickup from an xi motor to rwd
  • Bell housing bolt set
  • Shifter bushings
  • Oil pan gasket
  • Oil level sensor gasket
  • Water pump bolts
  • AC compressor bolts
  • Pressure plate bolt kit
  • Clutch and pressure plate
  • Engine flush
  • Oil pan baffle

Dressed the motor

Did this on an engine stand and basically moved all parts I wanted to keep to the new motor. By far what added the most time were the MILVS

Reinstalled

This took only like 6 hours. This was easy

Prime the engine

The last thing we did was plug in the injectors. We did that after we ran the starter 3x for 10s each with a 10s pause in between.

Ran the engine for 30min

Fully up to temp. Engine flush too

Changed the oil

Repeated the warmup

Got a deafening tick

It was so loud we needed hearing protection with the hood open. It was wild. Turned out to be a lifter. My motor was an 08/09 motor so it's an early one. Lifter additive resolved it after a few days.
