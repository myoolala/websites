Story · Drive

First mountain run with the new setup.

Date: Sep 01, 2025 Route: Blue Ridge-style backroads (example) Weather: 68°F · Clear Companions: Solo Setup: Coilovers · fresh tires
The day & the drive

A simple plan: take the freshly set up 3 Series out to some familiar roads, feel out the new suspension and tires, and see how everything behaves over a longer drive with elevation, tight corners, and rough sections of pavement.

Morning prep

09:00 – Garage

Checked fluids, torqued the wheel lugs, and did a quick walkaround to make sure nothing was rubbing or leaking after the last round of work. Threw basic tools, a tire pressure gauge, and an emergency kit in the trunk, then set the suspension to a slightly softer street setting than the last alignment day.

Optional photo: the car in the garage before heading out

Highway out & first impressions

10:00 – Outbound highway

The first stretch was mostly highway, just enough time to feel how the car behaved over expansion joints and mild bumps. Ride quality was firm but not harsh, with far less float than stock. Steering felt a bit more direct, and small corrections at speed needed less wheel input than before.

Note: logged a few minutes of data here — speed, steering angle, and ride height changes — to compare against alignment day runs.

Into the twisties

11:00 – Mountain section

Once the road tightened up, the differences really showed. Turn-in was sharper and the chassis settled quickly mid-corner. Bumps that used to unsettle the rear now just registered as a quick motion, with the car staying composed instead of bouncing. Braking zones felt more predictable with a more stable rear end.

Optional photo: overlook / car parked with the mountains in the background

Break at the overlook

12:15 – Overlook pull-off

Pulled off at a small overlook to let brakes cool and get out of the car for a minute. Popped the hood to check for any leaks, smells, or loose connections—everything looked clean. Took a few photos and made quick notes on tire pressures and how the car reacted in a couple of specific corners.

Road notes: corner 3 tight hairpin, slight off-camber; car stayed neutral with just a hint of safe understeer when pushing harder.

Return trip & takeaways

14:00 – Heading home

On the way back, the pace was calmer, focusing on consistency rather than speed. No strange noises, no rubbing, and the car stayed composed even over rough patches that normally upset the chassis. The setup feels daily-drivable while still clearly more capable than stock.

Takeaway: this is a solid baseline for the chassis. Next steps will be fine-tuning damper clicks and potentially adding a bit more front camber for longer track sessions.
