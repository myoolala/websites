335i brake conversion

Overview

What this mod is

So if you don't have a 335 or 135, this is for you to have a cheap option for a brake upgrade. Depending the car, this can be a direct bolt on. This is really intended for anyone doing a track day in their 128, 130, 328, or 330 as the stock brakes will be asked to do a lot by an experience driver.

For my e91 even doing just the front brakes, I was able to do a 50 minute session on track without any fade despite never going into pit.
Why do it

Benefits & tradeoffs

As said prior, this is to improve the thermal capacity of the brakes for track driving. There isn't any other real point to it, though 335i brakes under 17" rims do look great. Unlike the f80 brake swap, these will keep up just fine on track and are much cheaper to replace.
Planning

What to decide before you buy/install

So the fronts are simple just get the pads, rotors, and swap them on. The rear though... that's where it gets tricky. Basically, for e9x platforms the rear knuckle supports either a small or large handbrake. The 335i rotors kind of require the large handbrake which means replace the knuckle on e90, e92 328i rwd cars.

Now, an e90 328xi has the large handbrake, but as I found out the spacing to the hub is different so I couldn't bolt the brakes to it. Unsure if that's a part issue or something else. E91 328i rwd cars this is a direct swap though, just throw the rotors and brakes on.

Now if you do swap the knuckles, that also means you will need to swap axles as the larger knuckle runs a larger axle with more splines. Go to a junkyard and grab everything from a source car. This also applies to the 128 to 135i brakes

Now if you have a 128i and grab everyting from a 135i to swap on those brakes, you actually reduce the rear hub width by 10mm per side. This actually opens wheel options for you. Specifically, those delicious Apex ARC-8 with the nice dish fit on a square setup.

If you only plan to do the front, you will want to code the sport brakes into the car as the brake bias will be destroyed. Now, this also means the car is much more likely to go into ICE mode. If you aware of it but it has nearly caused a friend to crash my car on a backroad when it caught him off guard.
Brake gruide

What do you have and what would you need

E91 328i
No extra parts needs, 335i brakes bolt straight on
E82 128i
  • 135i rear knuckles
  • 135i rear speed sensors
  • 135i rear axles
  • 135i handbrake
E90/E92 328i rwd
  • 335i rear knuckles
  • 335i rear speed sensors
  • 335i rear axles
  • 335i handbrake
My thoughts on this
If you are going to track the car do it. I at first thought I could get away with just doing the fronts since I didn't know the rears bolted straight on. Do all 4. The added rear application and stopping power was night and day on track. With only the front brakes I had to be gentle and firm onto the brakes to maximize them, but with all 4 corners swapped I could just slam the brakes on track as the ramp up was great.

The pedal will get noticeably longer since the master cylinder is now smaller relative to the calipers. I have yet to care.
