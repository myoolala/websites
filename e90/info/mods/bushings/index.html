Bushings

Overview

What this mod is

For the non-m cars, there are a large number of bushings in the suspension to reduce NVH. M3's though have bearings for the front lower control arms as well the tow arms and rear upper control arms. The radius arms on the m3 are fluid filled while being solid on the non m cars.

Here it will be the other bushings we will discuss the other bushings.
Subframe bushings

Options

For non m cars, you have the most options here. Namely you can run bushing inserts which are by far the easiest to install and from what we saw provided immediately noticeable results.

M3 subframe bushings are another option. While they are rubber, they are solid from the from the factory. 2 bushings have to be inserted from the top and 2 from the bottom. This makes installation tricky

There is always poly, from what we heard these can degrade pretty fast but nothing has been confirmed. Easy to install though

And the nuclear option of solid metal bushings. Have fun for the race car.
Shifter bushing

Options

I only tried the poly option for the shifter. Added no extra vibration in the cabin but I can see the shifter shake more especially at idle.
My thoughts on this
The subframe bushings by far added the most noticeable impact. The rear will depend on the goal with the car

Meta info

  • Difficulty: Medium
  • Approx Time: 1-8 hours
  • Follow-up: None
