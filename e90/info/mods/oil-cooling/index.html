Overview

What this mod is

Why do it

Benefits & tradeoffs

Ok rant time, on track this is like the biggest issue with these cars. I don't understand how this isn't talked about more and only guess that since a lot of the n52 cars don't have an oil temp gauge it is missed. We have seen these cars go into limp mode on track due to oil temps. To me, if you plan to track a 328 or 335, an oil cooler is a must.

To add to that, there are tons of options.
Planning

What to decide before you buy/install

Honestly, budget, which car you have, and intended driving style might be the biggest factors. Like the 330i oil cooler is best for hot autocross since it is the only option that actively works when the car is parked. But high speed track will favor any other option. Going on track with a 3700lb e91 will tax the engine more than a 3100lb e82. Know what you are trying to do and what your goals are.
Oil Cooler Options

What could you buy

128i/328i to 330i water based oil cooler
This is the most factory option for the N52 cars. There are kits for this and it serves the largest array of capabilities. It cools the oil when up to temp to the lowest highway temp vs the 335i oil thermostat, it also warms the oil faster in the winter, provides the most cooling when parked, and takes the least space.

What sucks with this job is that you have to replace the metal coolant pipe so you will messing with the water pump. Have fun. But otherwise it's an easy job.

I ran this for one track weekend in september on a 75Â° day and saw 295Â° oil temp at the end of the main straight each lap. It would cool to 265Â° within a couple corners but man, that's knocking on the door to limp mode. I changed it oil that winter.
335i factory oil cooler
This is the other factory option which you can of course just go to a junkyard and grab all the parts for. This one will work better on track than the 330i cooler as the airflow will trump the coolant. The part that sucks here is installation requires remove the bumper but it's not that much work in reality. No idea how well these perform on track.
335i CSF oil cooler
All the same this as 335i factory cooler except a bigger radiator. All other parts are the same.
After market oil coolers
There are even bigger options after the CSF unit. I linked a Mosselman unit but you can use basically anything and mount them anywhere. The big thing to note is that now you might be running your own AN lines. It's not hard just another thing you would need to account for.
M3 oil cooler
This is the route I went after seeing my oil temps in September. This only works for the N52 cars as the M3 oil cooler mounts to the same place the intercooler mounts. But aside from that all of the factory m3 mounting hardware works, you will just need to make your own AN lines. In March I saw 230 peak temps at 50Â° outside and 250 peak temps on an sunny 85Â° day in May. Very happy with this.
